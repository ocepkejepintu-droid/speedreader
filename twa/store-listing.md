## Full description (max 4000 chars)

```
RSVP Reader is a focused, distraction-free speed-reading app for books and
articles. It shows you one word at a time, anchored at the Optimal
Recognition Point, so your eyes don't have to move. You set the pace — from
200 to 1000 words per minute — and the app keeps the focus exactly where it
belongs: on the next word.

WHY IT WORKS
Most readers read by moving their eyes across a line, then saccading back.
Each saccade is a micro-interruption. RSVP (Rapid Serial Visual Presentation)
removes the interruption: words appear, one at a time, centered on a single
spot. The Optimal Recognition Point — the slight left-of-center letter
position your brain actually uses to recognise a word — is highlighted in
red. Your peripheral vision still picks up context, but your eyes stay put.

WHAT YOU CAN READ
- EPUBs from your own library
- Plain text and Markdown files
- Articles you share from any app (the share target catches them)
- Long-form web articles you paste in

EVERY FEATURE YOU EXPECT
- Library with progress, last-read, and a per-book WPM
- Bookmark and resume across devices (anonymous, end-to-end sync token)
- Adjustable WPM, font size, and accent color
- Light and dark themes
- Offline by default — your books live in your browser
- No account, no email, no phone number, no tracking
- Installable PWA on iOS, Android, and desktop
- Free, MIT-licensed, no in-app purchases

DESIGNED FOR FOCUS
No streaks. No notifications. No "you've been on the app for 3 hours" guilt
loops. No ads. No "follow the author". Just a single word, in the middle of
the screen, waiting for you to be ready for the next one.

Built by a single developer who wanted to read more books this year and
fewer infinite feeds. Open source: github.com/zipang/rsvp-reader.
```

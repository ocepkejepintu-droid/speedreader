# Show HN: RSVP Reader — a one-word-at-a-time EPUB reader in your browser

I built a free, no-account EPUB reader that flashes one word at a time on the same focal point, so your eyes never have to move. Library and progress stay on your device and sync through iCloud Drive — no server, no signup, no telemetry.

I made it because I kept buying books and never finishing them in transit. The 30-minute train window was too short to get into a chapter and too long to waste on a feed. RSVP Nano proved the format works on hardware, but I didn't want to carry another device, so I built the software twin as a PWA.

Three things it does: ORP-anchored RSVP (the red letter is the focal character, saccades are zero), a Phantom mode that shows context around the current word, and a plain scroll mode for when you want to skim. Open the same URL on your phone and the library is there.

No account, no tracking, free. EPUBs are yours. Progress is yours. The sync server only relays encrypted blobs you could read yourself in DevTools.
